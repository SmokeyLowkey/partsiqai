## What to Look for: The Essential Feature Checklist

**Before comparing specific products, understand what features actually move the needle** for parts-intensive operations.

### Must-Have Features

- **Real-time stock tracking** — Every part movement (receipt, issue, transfer, return) tracked instantly across all locations
- **Multi-location support** — Centralized visibility across yards, warehouses, and job sites
- **Automated reorder alerts** — Configurable min/max levels with automatic notifications
- **Barcode/QR scanning** — Mobile scanning for receiving, issuing, and counting parts
- **Reporting and analytics** — Usage reports, cost analysis, inventory health dashboards
- **Mobile access** — Technicians need to check stock and request parts from the field

### Differentiating Features

- **AI-powered search** — Natural language queries instead of requiring exact part numbers
- **Cross-reference databases** — OEM to aftermarket part mapping
- **Supplier management** — Integrated RFQ distribution and quote comparison
- **Procurement automation** — System-generated purchase orders from reorder alerts
- **Maintenance integration** — PM schedules driving parts forecasting
- **API integrations** — Connections to your ERP, CMMS, and accounting systems

### Nice-to-Have Features

- **Predictive demand modeling** — AI forecasting based on usage patterns
- **Image-based part identification** — Photo lookup
- **Consignment inventory tracking** — Vendor-managed inventory support
- **Multi-currency support** — For operations with international suppliers

### Sortly

**Best for:** Small shops and operations that need simple, visual inventory tracking without complex procurement features.

Sortly is a clean, user-friendly inventory app that works well for small operations with fewer than 1,000 SKUs. It excels at visual tracking with photograph capability for every part and organization through tags and custom fields. QR code labels and mobile scanning make it easy to track items.

**Strengths:**
- Very intuitive interface, minimal training needed
- Strong mobile app with photo capability
- Affordable entry point

**Considerations:**
- No AI search or cross-referencing
- No supplier management or procurement automation
- Limited reporting capabilities
- Doesn't scale well beyond a few thousand SKUs

### Fishbowl

**Best for:** Manufacturing operations that need inventory management tightly integrated with QuickBooks or Xero.

Fishbowl has been a mainstay in inventory management for manufacturing, with strong warehouse management features including pick/pack/ship workflows, multi-location transfers, and lot tracking. Its deep QuickBooks integration is a standout for small to mid-size manufacturers.

**Strengths:**
- Excellent QuickBooks and Xero integration
- Strong warehouse management features
- Manufacturing-specific features (BOM, work orders)

**Considerations:**
- No AI search capability
- Limited supplier management
- Interface feels dated compared to newer solutions
- Pricing can escalate quickly with add-ons

### Limble CMMS

**Best for:** Maintenance-first organizations where parts inventory is secondary to work order management.

Limble is primarily a CMMS (Computerized Maintenance Management System) with parts inventory as a supporting feature. If your primary need is tracking work orders and maintenance schedules, and you want parts management bundled in, Limble delivers a clean experience.

**Strengths:**
- Excellent work order management
- Parts tied directly to maintenance tasks
- Good preventive maintenance scheduling

**Considerations:**
- Parts inventory is a secondary feature, not the core product
- No AI search or cross-referencing
- Limited procurement automation

### UpKeep

**Best for:** Mobile-first maintenance teams that need work order management with basic parts tracking.

UpKeep is similar to Limble in positioning — a CMMS with parts inventory built in. Its mobile app is particularly strong, making it popular with field service teams. Parts can be linked to assets and work orders for consumption tracking.

**Strengths:**
- Best-in-class mobile app for maintenance teams

**Considerations:**
- Parts management is lightweight compared to dedicated solutions
- No AI search capabilities

### Fleetio

**Best for:** Vehicle fleet operations (trucks, service vehicles, delivery fleets) that need parts tracking alongside fleet management.

Fleetio combines fleet management (vehicle tracking, fuel management, compliance) with parts inventory and service management. For vehicle-focused operations, having everything in one platform reduces complexity.

**Strengths:**
- Integrated fleet + parts management
- Per-vehicle pricing model is cost-effective
- Good fuel management and compliance features

**Considerations:**
- Designed for vehicle fleets, not heavy equipment
- No AI-powered parts search
- Not ideal for operations with diverse equipment types

### EZOfficeInventory

**Best for:** Asset-heavy organizations that need to track both parts/consumables and capital equipment.

EZOfficeInventory shines when you need unified tracking of assets (equipment, tools, vehicles) alongside parts and consumables. Features like asset depreciation, checkout/check-in, and maintenance scheduling make it comprehensive.

**Strengths:**
- Combined asset and inventory tracking
- Good checkout/reservation workflows
- Integrations with Zendesk, Jira, and Slack

**Considerations:**
- Not specialized for industrial parts
- No AI search or cross-referencing
- Limited procurement automation

## Best For: By Industry

### Heavy Equipment and Construction
**Top picks:** PartsIQ, Fleetio (for vehicle-focused fleets)

Construction operations deal with multi-brand equipment fleets, thousands of unique SKUs, and the highest cost of downtime. AI-powered search and cross-referencing capabilities are most valuable here.

### HVAC and Mechanical Contractors
**Top picks:** Sortly, Fishbowl

HVAC operations typically have moderate SKU counts with fairly standard parts across brands. Sortly's simplicity works for smaller shops, while Fishbowl's warehouse features suit larger distributors.

### Manufacturing
**Top picks:** Fishbowl, EZOfficeInventory

Manufacturing needs differ — BOM management, lot tracking, and accounting integration matter more than AI search. Fishbowl's QuickBooks integration and manufacturing features make it a natural fit.

### General Fleet and Field Service
**Top picks:** Fleetio, UpKeep

Vehicle fleets and field service operations benefit from integrated fleet management + parts tracking. Fleetio's per-vehicle pricing and UpKeep's mobile-first approach both serve this segment well.

## How to Evaluate: A Decision Framework

<StepList>
  <Step number={1} title="Define Your Must-Haves vs Nice-to-Haves">
    Use the feature checklist above. Be honest about what you actually need today versus what sounds nice. A simpler solution you'll actually use beats a powerful one that collects dust.
  </Step>
  <Step number={2} title="Trial at Least 3 Solutions">
    Most platforms offer free trials or demos. Test them with real scenarios your team faces daily: finding a part, checking stock, processing a reorder.
  </Step>
  <Step number={3} title="Test with Real Inventory Data">
    Import a sample of your actual parts data during the trial. A tool that works great with demo data might struggle with your 8,000 SKUs across 3 locations.
  </Step>
  <Step number={4} title="Evaluate Team Adoption">
    The best software is the one your team will use. Have your technicians, parts staff, and procurement team test each option. Their feedback matters more than feature lists.
  </Step>
  <Step number={5} title="Calculate ROI Before Committing">
    Estimate your current costs: time spent searching for parts, emergency order premiums, stockout-related downtime, excess inventory carrying costs. Then model the improvement each platform could deliver. The right solution should pay for itself within 3-6 months.
  </Step>
</StepList>
